About uSurvey

Introduction

uSurvey is a web-based data collection tool designed in partnership with the Uganda Bureau of Statistics (UBOS) to

  1. Enhance timely release of survey results,
  2. Increase frequency of collection of information on indicators that would otherwise be updated after long time intervals, (e.g. every 4-5 years); and
  3. Facilitatethe collection of more disaggregated data (i.e. smaller geographical units), thereby enabling the identification and collection of information on marginalized communities.

USSD and ODK Channel

uSurvey enables real-time data collection and storage using mobile device (tablet/phone) through via USSD and ODK channels. This secure and interactive connection enables communication through the relay of survey questions from the server to the hand-held mobile device and the instant transmission of each response from the mobile device to the server. The off-line ODK (Open Data Kit) based mobile application to optimize its utilization in locations with intermittent mobile connections.

Sampled and Non Sampled Survey

uSurvey has been developed to collect information from a scientifically selected sample in standard surveys. To this end, it possesses in-built capabilities to randomly select a representative sample from a population of interest and generate a list of unique identification numbers for each sampling unit (e.g. households, institutions, etc.) to be included in the sample. Just like for traditional surveys techniques, uSurvey results can be used to make inferences about the population from which the sample was selected.

uSurvey has an in-built “questions bank” to enable the speedy design of survey questionnaires through selection of applicable questions, with automated validation and skip logic, for each survey instead of typing them every time they are needed for a survey. Further, interviewers/enumerators can be appointed in advance and assigned to his/her Enumeration Area (EA) by phone number. The system will automatically assign the data from that phone number to the EA. The survey can be launched centrally to collect data over a USSD channel or by downloading the survey on any smartphone.



Real Time Monitoring:

uSurvey eliminates the use of paper questionnaires and associated delays in the release of survey results oftentimes due to the time required for data capture, editing and cleaning. The tool enables remote review and (if necessary) editing of captured data in real time, while the data collectors are still in the field. This feature enables the use of smaller field teams through the removal of field-based editors. In addition, the system has in-built data summarization and visualization features and is, therefore, capable to produce graphic or tabulated descriptive statistics such as frequencies, proportions, means, basic charts and distribution maps for variables selected by the user.

To ensure that the information entered into the system was captured from the correct sampling unit, uSurvey captures sampling units geo-codes as well as the time when the enumeration of each sampling unit was done. The tool also has in-built automated edit checks that can be selected in association with questions to ensure that only valid responses are captured against each question. This caters for edit checks typically built in programmes developed for survey data capture when paper questionnaires are used for data collection during surveys.

uSurvey will significantly contribute to the Agenda 2030 requirement of a data revolution to provide evidence for development programming, and enable the monitoring of SDG indicators, by implementing one of the three recommendations of the UN Secretary-General appointed Independent Expert Advisory Group on a Data Revolution for Sustainable Development (IEAG): Fostering and promoting innovation to fill data gaps. This tool is expected to improve the quality of survey data through: (i) shortened time lag between surveydata collection and results release, (ii) increased levels of geographical disaggregation of collected data, and (iii) increased frequency of survey data collection. Furthermore, uSurvey will contribute to addressing the two big global challenges highlighted with regards to the current state of data by IEAG:

The challenge of invisibility
The challenge of inequality
